Private Villa Rental Near Athens for Large Groups: The Complete Guide

February 2026 · 10 min read
Traveling to Greece with a large group sounds magical until you start searching for accommodation. Hotels scatter your party across different floors and buildings. Airbnb listings cap out at 8 or 10 guests. And the villas that do accommodate 15 or more people tend to be either overpriced, poorly located, or both. Finding a private villa rental near Athens that genuinely works for large groups requires knowing where to look and what to prioritize.

Why Large Groups Need a Different Approach to Greek Accommodation

The standard Greek holiday formula breaks down once your group exceeds 10 people. Booking three or four separate hotel rooms means your group never feels like a group. You eat breakfast at different times, rely on messaging apps to coordinate, and spend half your holiday waiting for someone to get ready in a different building. The shared spaces that define great group holidays, a big dining table, a communal pool area, a living room where everyone gathers in the evening, simply don't exist in a hotel context.

Short-term rental platforms have improved options for mid-sized groups, but most listings that claim to accommodate 15 or more guests achieve that number by filling every bedroom and sofa bed to capacity. The result is a villa that technically sleeps 16 but feels cramped with 12. Bathrooms become bottlenecks. Kitchen space disappears. The privacy that makes villa holidays appealing evaporates when bedrooms share thin walls and everyone competes for the same two bathrooms.

A properly designed large-group villa operates differently. It provides genuine living space, not just sleeping space. Multiple common areas allow the group to split naturally: some by the pool, others reading in the shade, a few preparing lunch together. When evening comes, everyone converges in a space built for communal dining, and the villa feels alive without feeling overcrowded.

The Athens Advantage for Large Group Holidays

When most travelers think of Greece, they picture Santorini sunsets and Mykonos beaches. But for large groups, the islands present logistical challenges that multiply with every additional guest. Inter-island ferries have limited vehicle space, making group transportation complicated. Island villa options for 15 or more guests are scarce and disproportionately expensive. And the tourism infrastructure on smaller islands can struggle to accommodate large party needs, from restaurant reservations to activity bookings.

The Athens region solves these problems comprehensively. Athens International Airport receives direct flights from virtually every European capital and most major international cities, eliminating the need for domestic transfers. Ground transportation is straightforward: rent two minivans and your entire group moves together. The mainland's road network connects you to archaeological sites, beaches, restaurants, and activities without ferry schedules dictating your plans.

Cost efficiency improves dramatically on the mainland. A comparable villa on Mykonos or Santorini commands a nightly rate two to three times higher than an equivalent property in the Athens Riviera. When you're renting for a week with 15 or more guests, that price differential translates into thousands of euros in savings, money better spent on experiences, meals, and excursions that make the holiday memorable.

Porto Rafti: Where Large Group Villas Meet Authentic Greece

Among the Athens Riviera's coastal towns, Porto Rafti occupies a unique position for large group holidays. Located 30 kilometers southeast of Athens and just 16 kilometers from the airport, it offers a combination that few Greek destinations can match: proximity to Athens's cultural attractions, a genuine local character that hasn't been diluted by mass tourism, and a coastal landscape of protected bays, clear waters, and panoramic hillside views.

Porto Rafti's geography works in favor of group accommodations. The hillsides surrounding the bay provide elevated building plots where villas enjoy sea views and cooling breezes without being stacked on top of each other. Properties here tend to sit on larger plots than island villas, providing the outdoor space that large groups need: gardens, terraces, and pool areas where 20 people can spread out comfortably.

The town itself supports group holidays with practical infrastructure that tourists rarely think about until they need it. Supermarkets stock supplies for large-group cooking. Several tavernas welcome big parties without requiring weeks of advance booking. A medical center provides peace of mind for families with young children or elderly relatives. Mobile phone coverage and internet connectivity are reliable, important for groups coordinating activities or remote workers extending their stay.

What to Look for in a Large-Group Villa Near Athens

Not every villa that advertises high guest capacity actually delivers a comfortable experience for large groups. Here are the factors that separate genuinely suitable properties from those merely padding their occupancy numbers.

Bedroom-to-bathroom ratio: This single metric reveals more about a villa's suitability for large groups than any marketing description. A property with 8 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms will cause daily frustration. Look for a minimum ratio of one bathroom for every two bedrooms, and ideally en-suite bathrooms for the majority of rooms. Morning routines with 15 or more guests require adequate facilities, and no amount of sea views compensates for queuing for the shower.

Indoor common space: Greece offers spectacular outdoor living, but even in summer, groups need indoor gathering areas. Rain happens. Midday heat drives people inside. Evening entertainment shifts to living rooms. A villa suited for 20 guests should provide at least 100 square meters of indoor common space beyond the bedrooms. This typically means a spacious living room, a dining area that seats the full group, and a kitchen where multiple people can work simultaneously.

Outdoor area diversity: The best large-group villas provide multiple distinct outdoor zones rather than a single terrace. A pool area, a shaded dining terrace, a garden lounge area, and perhaps a sports or games space allow the group to function as several smaller groups throughout the day, reuniting for meals and shared activities. This spatial diversity prevents the feeling of being constantly in each other's space, which can strain even the closest friendships over a week-long stay.

Kitchen capacity: Large-group cooking is one of the great pleasures of villa holidays, but only if the kitchen can handle it. Look for double-sized refrigerators (or two separate units), ample counter space, a full-sized oven, and multiple cooking hobs. A villa that accommodates 20 guests but provides a kitchen designed for a family of four creates an unnecessary bottleneck.

Parking and access: Practical but often overlooked. A group of 20 typically arrives in 3 or 4 vehicles. Verify that the property offers adequate parking and that the access road can handle larger vehicles. Some hillside villas feature narrow roads that become stressful with a loaded minivan.

Planning a Large Group Holiday: Logistics That Matter

Transportation strategy: For groups of 15 to 22, renting two or three vehicles provides the most flexibility. Athens airport car rental agencies offer competitive rates, and having multiple vehicles allows the group to split for different activities. Some prefer a full-day Athens sightseeing trip while others want a beach day, and separate vehicles make both possible without forcing consensus on every plan.

Grocery and provisioning: Large-group villas near Athens benefit from mainland supply chains. Porto Rafti's supermarkets stock everything a group needs, from fresh produce to specific dietary requirements. For arrival-day convenience, several local services offer pre-stocking: your villa's kitchen is fully provisioned when you walk through the door, eliminating the chaotic first-day supermarket run with 20 hungry travelers.

Activity coordination: The Athens region offers activity options that scale well for large groups. Private boat charters from nearby marinas accommodate groups for coastal exploration. Wine tasting tours in the Attica region welcome parties of 15 or more. Cooking classes, archaeological tours, and water sports all function better near Athens, where operators are accustomed to handling large bookings and multilingual groups.

Budgeting per person: One of the most compelling arguments for large-group villa holidays is the per-person cost. A villa that costs 800 euros per night sounds expensive until you divide it by 20 guests: 40 euros per person per night for luxury accommodation with a private pool, sea views, and complete privacy. Compare that to 150 or more euros per person for a comparable hotel experience, and the villa's value proposition becomes overwhelming.

Comparing Villa Regions Near Athens

The Athens Riviera stretches from the southern suburbs of Athens along the coast to Cape Sounion. Each area offers different characteristics for large-group stays.

Glyfada and Vouliagmeni: Closest to Athens city center (20 to 30 minutes), these upscale suburbs offer sophisticated dining and shopping. However, villas here tend to sit on smaller plots with less privacy, and rental prices reflect the premium location. Best suited for groups that prioritize Athens nightlife and urban amenities over space and seclusion.

Lagonissi and Saronida: The mid-Riviera towns (40 to 50 minutes from the airport) offer a balance of beach access and reasonable property sizes. Villa options for large groups exist but are limited, and the area can feel suburban rather than distinctively Greek.

Porto Rafti and Vravrona: Further east (15 to 20 minutes from the airport), these towns combine the largest property plots with the most authentic Greek character. Porto Rafti in particular offers dramatic hillside settings with sea views, a protected bay for swimming, and a local community that hasn't been transformed by tourism. For large groups seeking space, privacy, and genuine Greece, this area delivers the strongest combination.

Cape Sounion area: The southeastern tip (60 to 70 minutes from the airport) offers spectacular scenery near the Temple of Poseidon. However, the distance from the airport and limited local services make it less practical for large groups, particularly those with children or elderly members who need convenient access to amenities.

Booking Timeline and Seasonal Pricing

Large-group villas near Athens follow predictable booking patterns. Properties that genuinely accommodate 15 or more guests are relatively rare, meaning the best options book months in advance for peak season.

Peak season (July and August) commands the highest rates and requires booking 4 to 6 months ahead for desirable properties. Shoulder seasons (May to June and September to October) offer the best value: lower rates, fewer crowds, and weather that many find more comfortable than the intense midsummer heat. Spring bookings benefit from wildflower landscapes and pleasant 22 to 27 degree temperatures, while September offers warm seas from summer heating and golden evening light that photographers love.

For cost-conscious large groups, consider week-long stays, which most villa owners discount by 10 to 15 percent compared to the per-night rate. Some properties also offer early booking discounts for reservations made 6 or more months ahead. Combined, these savings can reduce the per-person cost to remarkably affordable levels for what amounts to luxury accommodation.

It combines airport proximity, mainland infrastructure, authentic Greek character, and properties built for the scale that large groups require. When your extended family, friend group, or celebration party can walk through the door of a villa that genuinely fits everyone, the holiday begins the moment you arrive rather than three days in, once the logistics have finally been sorted.
